My Experience

Constituent Services Intern

During my first year at Pitt, I found that I was deeply interested in policy. Following my first year at the University of Pittsburgh, I explored my budding interest in policy as an intern at the office of Congressman Paul Tonko in Albany, New York. In this role, I responded to constituent inquiries by liaising with federal agencies. I reviewed my supervisor’s entire caseload for additional inquiries and organized outreach and tabling events. I credit this role with being the catalyst to my future path, allowing me exposure into the powering role of government in creating vital connections to public services. I am grateful to this day for the office’s willingness to have me come on board as a freshman in university.
